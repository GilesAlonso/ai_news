Global Controversies Ignite X: India’s Parliament erupts over alleged “vote chori” deception, Trump own admission of “shithole countries” remark, and Congress brawl over the alleged “Bolsonaro Law”

Published on 2025-12-11

World

  • India’s Parliament erupts over alleged “vote chori” deception
    Rahul Gandhi challenged Amit Shah to debate supposed electoral fraud and accused the BJP of running a fake-news factory across three recent press conferences, sparking viral distrust ahead of the elections.
  • Chile’s #DebateAnatel2025 ignites fact-check fury
    Jeannette Jara and other critics accused José Antonio Kast of fabrications on pensions and anti-discrimination laws, prompting La Tercera to publish a list of debate lies that still divide voters.
  • UK immigration clash over digital IDs
    A parliamentary debate turned explosive as MPs used high-profile migrant crime reports to attack the government’s plans for mandatory digital identification, fueling petitions and polarized coverage.

USA

  • Trump own admission of “shithole countries” remark
    In a speech, the president confirmed he used the phrase in 2018, reigniting outrage over racism in his immigration policy and drawing millions of views on X.
  • Somali welfare fraud probe vs. Ilhan Omar
    The administration told a prosecutor to chase what it called the “single greatest theft” while Omar and critics accused it of racially targeting Minnesota’s Somali community and playing to xenophobic sentiment.
  • Transgender rights and school policies under fire
    Lawsuits and DOJ action spread from Loudoun to Michigan and Maryland, with conservatives calling it “woke indoctrination” and liberals warning of rights rollbacks.

Brazil

  • Congress brawl over the alleged “Bolsonaro Law”
    Opposition lawmakers tried to soften coup-related penalties to reduce Bolsonaro’s sentence, forcing physical removals and dramatic headlines about democracy being undermined.
  • Datena appointee sparks media backlash
    Critics say Lula’s pick of sensationalist host José Luiz Datena for state media shows contradicts the broadcaster’s mission and revives debates on bias and favoritism.
  • Airline seat dispute turns into lawsuit
    A woman who refused to give up her paid window seat for a crying child went viral, now plans to sue the airline and the filament passenger for allegedly ruining her life.
