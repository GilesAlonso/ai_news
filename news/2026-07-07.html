World

  • FIFA suspends red-card ban for US striker after reported Trump call to Infantino
    FIFA suspended the automatic one-match ban issued to US striker Folarin Balogun following a disputed VAR red card in the US win over Bosnia, allowing him to play in the Round of 16 against Belgium. Reports indicate that US President Donald Trump contacted FIFA President Gianni Infantino to request a review; FIFA stated the judicial bodies acted independently. Belgium and other participants publicly objected to the process, alleging political interference in sporting decisions.

    Belgorod international airport in Russia reported an attack by Ukrainian forces, with explosions reported on site. The incident occurred amid ongoing Russia-Ukraine conflict developments, with both sides continuing to exchange strikes across border regions.

    California Governor Gavin Newsom announced legislation that would classify seizure of ballots before certification by state and county officials as a felony.     Senator Ron Johnson stated that Republicans should end the Senate filibuster to pass the SAVE Act and 'restore integrity and confidence' in federal elections. The proposal triggered debate over voter ID requirements, federal election standards, and the procedural implications of eliminating the filibuster.

The remarks fueled debate over the connection between corporate tax policy and employment or consumer prices.

    Representative Ruben Gallego publicly rescinded his endorsement of Senate candidate Graham Platner, citing allegations he described as 'troubling and deeply serious.' The announcement generated debate over vetting processes and party support for candidates facing allegations.
