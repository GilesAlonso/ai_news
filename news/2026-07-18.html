The Daily Globe: U.S. reports another round of strikes on Iran, Wildfire smoke, floods and new fires affect multiple U.S. regions, and U.S. announces 25% tariff on selected Brazilian imports

Published on 2026-07-18

World

  • U.S. reports another round of strikes on Iran
    The U.S. military said on July 17 that it completed a new wave of strikes on Iran, the seventh consecutive night of reported attacks; Iran also reported attacks involving Gulf states and shipping routes, according to Reuters.
  • Magnitude-7.3 earthquake strikes off Mexico's Chiapas coast
    A magnitude-7.3 earthquake struck near Mexico's southern coast on July 17, prompting a tsunami warning and shaking buildings in Guatemala and El Salvador, according to the U.S. Geological Survey and Reuters.
  • Asian technology shares fall in global market selloff
    Asian equity benchmarks, including markets in Japan and Taiwan, fell sharply on July 17 as a technology-stock correction extended across global markets, Reuters reported.

USA

  • Wildfire smoke, floods and new fires affect multiple U.S. regions
    On July 17, wildfire smoke reached from the Great Lakes to Washington, D.C., flooding continued in Texas Hill Country, and new fires were reported in the Pacific Northwest; Reuters counted 68 large fires in 15 states.
  • SpaceX targets another Starship launch after abort
    SpaceX said it was targeting a new Starship launch attempt after a July 16 engine-ignition abort; Reuters reported that the incident reduced the company's market value by about $100 billion.
  • Protests accompany U.S. ambassador's visit to Venice
    Demonstrators gathered in Venice on July 17 as Ambassador Tilman Fertitta arrived by yacht; protesters criticized U.S. policies and the use of police resources, Reuters and AP reported.

Brazil

  • U.S. announces 25% tariff on selected Brazilian imports
    The United States said a 25% tariff on Brazilian imports would begin on July 22, citing what it described as unfair trade practices; the measure became a central bilateral dispute.
  • Brazil says it may impose reciprocal tariffs
    Brazilian officials called the U.S. tariff unjustifiable and said the country could impose reciprocal measures while maintaining negotiations, according to AP.
  • Congress begins recess without voting on several debated proposals
    Brazil's Congress entered recess on July 17 without voting on proposals involving the six-day workweek, artificial-intelligence regulation, public security and other issues, Folha reported.
