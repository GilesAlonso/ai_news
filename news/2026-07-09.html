The Daily Globe: European Parliament set to revisit 'Chat Control' mass-surveillance bill, Maine Senate candidate Graham Platner faces sexual assault allegations, and Senate accused of delaying 'end of 6x1' work-schedule bill

Published on 2026-07-09

World

  • European Parliament set to revisit 'Chat Control' mass-surveillance bill
    The European Parliament is preparing a second vote on the EU 'Chat Control' proposal, which would require platforms to scan private messages. The proposal was rejected in March and is now being reintroduced under urgency procedures in July, drawing criticism from digital-rights groups.
  • Turkey accelerates trial of Istanbul opposition mayor Ekrem İmamoğlu
    A Turkish court has expedited hearings in the case against jailed Istanbul mayor and opposition figure Ekrem İmamoğlu, with proceedings continuing without the defendant present. Critics frame the timing as politically motivated; the government maintains the charges are judicial.
  • UK Labour ministers face backlash over social media regulation plans
    Senior UK Labour figures, including Lisa Nandy and Lucy Powell, are advancing proposals to regulate online political content and prioritize 'trustworthy' sources. Free-speech organizations argue the measures could restrict lawful political debate.
  • France: petition against 'presumption of legitimate defense' for police passes threshold
    A French National Assembly petition opposing a bill introducing a presumption of legitimate defense for police officers in shootings has gathered enough signatures to trigger a new parliamentary debate. The proposal would shift the burden of proof toward victims' families.

USA

  • Maine Senate candidate Graham Platner faces sexual assault allegations
    Democratic U.S. Senate candidate Graham Platner, running in Maine, is facing renewed scrutiny following sexual assault allegations. The controversy has triggered internal party debate over whether to field a replacement candidate before the election.
  • Trump renews public disputes with European leaders at NATO Summit
    President Donald Trump used the NATO Summit to renew public criticisms of allied governments and to mock California Governor Gavin Newsom. The remarks have drawn both domestic political reactions and commentary from European officials.
  • DNA development reported in Charlie Kirk investigation
    Investigators in the Charlie Kirk case have disclosed new DNA-related findings, according to coverage on July 8. Details have not been publicly released, and the development is generating partisan reactions online.
  • Maryland moves toward redistricting ahead of 2028 cycle
    Maryland legislators are advancing a redistricting process that could reshape congressional maps before the 2028 elections. The move is drawing both support and criticism along partisan lines.

Brazil

  • Senate accused of delaying 'end of 6x1' work-schedule bill
    Senator Davi Alcolumbre is facing accusations from opposition figures of striking a deal to postpone the bill ending the 6x1 workweek until after the elections. The Senate president has not publicly confirmed any such agreement; supporters of the bill are mobilizing public pressure.
  • Flávio Bolsonaro faces renewed allegations of ties to organized crime
    Senator Flávio Bolsonaro is again the subject of public allegations linking him to militia activity and the Comando Vermelho criminal faction, referenced in a viral list of Rio de Janeiro politicians. Bolsonaro has previously denied the accusations; no new charges have been filed in the cited posts.
  • STF releases individuals sanctioned by the United States
    Brazil's Supreme Federal Tribunal (STF) has ordered the release of individuals subject to U.S. Treasury sanctions for alleged money laundering linked to the PCC. The decision is fueling debate over judicial independence, sanctions policy, and Brazil-U.S. relations.
  • CCJ approves bill capping IPVA vehicle tax at 1% of vehicle value
    Brazil's Constitution and Justice Committee (CCJ) approved a bill that would cap the IPVA vehicle tax at 1% of the vehicle's value. The proposal is being framed by its sponsors as tax relief; the executive branch has not yet indicated a position.
