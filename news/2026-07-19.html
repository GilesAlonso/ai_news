The Daily Globe: US and Iran exchange strikes as shipping through Strait of Hormuz declines, US and Iran conflict raises risks for American forces and energy markets, and Brazil prepares response to US 25% tariff on imports

Published on 2026-07-19

World

  • US and Iran exchange strikes as shipping through Strait of Hormuz declines
    The United States and Iran exchanged strikes on July 18, while Bahrain reported air-raid sirens and shipping crossings through the Strait of Hormuz fell to a three-week low. Iranian authorities reported casualties from recent US strikes; those figures were not independently verified in the report.
  • Landslide in southwest China leaves at least eight dead and dozens missing
    A landslide in a county of Chongqing killed at least eight people and left dozens missing on July 18, according to China’s state broadcaster CCTV as reported by Reuters.
  • Ukrainians protest for a second day and call for a change in army leadership
    Protesters in Ukraine continued demonstrations on July 18 and called for the replacement of the army chief, while Russian strikes reportedly killed at least two people in Odesa, according to Reuters video coverage.

USA

  • US and Iran conflict raises risks for American forces and energy markets
    The United States continued strikes against Iran on July 18 as Iran reported attacks on US-linked positions; AP reported that two US troops were dead and one was missing after an Iranian attack on a base in Jordan.
  • Andrew and Tristan Tate arrested in Miami on a UK extradition request
    US Marshals arrested the social-media influencers in Miami on July 18 in connection with an extradition request from the United Kingdom, according to a person cited by AP.
  • Cuban dissident artist arrives in the United States after release from prison
    Luis Manuel Otero Alcántara arrived in Miami on July 18 after serving a five-year sentence in Cuba and being released on the condition that he leave the country, according to AP.

Brazil

  • Brazil prepares response to US 25% tariff on imports
    The United States announced a 25% tariff on certain Brazilian imports, scheduled to begin July 22; Brazil described the measure as unjustifiable and said it could impose reciprocal tariffs, according to AP.
  • Brazilian Chamber debates proposal to reduce the age of criminal responsibility
    The Chamber of Deputies debated a constitutional proposal concerning the age of criminal responsibility on July 18. The measure drew disagreement over its legal scope and potential effects on adolescents.
  • Shooting at convenience store in Paraná leaves three dead
    A shooting at a convenience store in northern Paraná left three people dead on July 18, according to the state public-security department as reported by G1.
