The Daily Globe: India youth protests continue over examination-paper leaks, Defense secretary faces Senate questions over Iran war costs, and New U.S. tariffs on Brazilian exports take effect

Published on 2026-07-23

World

  • India youth protests continue over examination-paper leaks
    Thousands of protesters remained in New Delhi and some clashed briefly with police while demanding Education Minister Dharmendra Pradhan's resignation over leaks reported to have affected about 2 million students.
  • Iran conflict raises regional security and energy concerns
    Reports described missile alerts in Jordan and continuing disruption around the Strait of Hormuz, while the United States and regional governments assessed the military and economic effects of the conflict.
  • Louvre reopens Apollo Gallery after crown-jewel theft
    The Paris gallery reopened after an estimated 88 million euros in crown jewels were stolen in a daytime heist; the stolen items had not been recovered and were not on display.

USA

  • Defense secretary faces Senate questions over Iran war costs
    Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth was questioned on Capitol Hill as he estimated that the Iran war had cost the United States about $37.5 billion.
  • Administration defers more than $1 billion in Medicaid payments
    The Trump administration said it was deferring more than $1 billion in Medicaid payments to California and Minnesota, linking the action to allegations of fraud in mostly Democratic-led states.
  • Senate panel advances restrictions on Chinese-linked vehicles
    The Senate Commerce Committee approved legislation that would tighten restrictions on automakers with more than 15% Chinese ownership, a threshold that could affect some global manufacturers.

Brazil

  • New U.S. tariffs on Brazilian exports take effect
    Additional U.S. tariffs on parts of Brazil's exports entered into force, raising Brazil's average U.S. import tariff to about 18.17%; the measure intensified debate over trade and diplomatic relations.
  • Brazil announces R$18.5 billion credit program for tariff-affected firms
    President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva announced credit for companies affected by the U.S. tariffs and the Middle East conflict; the government estimates about 2,400 companies are exposed.
  • Vale shareholders elect new board chairman
    Shareholders elected Manuel Lino Oliveira as Vale's board chair, in a decision backed by major shareholder Previ after weeks of public disagreement over the miner's governance.
