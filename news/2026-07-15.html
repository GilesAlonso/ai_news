The Daily Globe: European leaders use Bastille Day parade to signal support for Ukraine, ICE pauses most vehicle stops after two fatal shootings, and Government studies reciprocal measures in response to possible U.S. tariffs

Published on 2026-07-15

World

  • European leaders use Bastille Day parade to signal support for Ukraine
    France's July 14 parade included Ukrainian troops and aircraft from European countries, presenting political and military support for Kyiv while the war with Russia continued.
  • Russian and Ukrainian claims follow attacks affecting Sea of Azov shipping
    Russia accused Ukraine of terrorism after attacks on shipping in the Sea of Azov; Reuters reported restrictions on a route used for grain exports.
  • Fighting in Gaza leaves at least 10 Palestinians dead, officials say
    Gazan health and police officials said Israeli fire and strikes killed at least 10 Palestinians, including a child, on July 14; the reported figures were not independently verified in the report.

USA

  • ICE pauses most vehicle stops after two fatal shootings
    U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement temporarily suspended most vehicle stops after agents fatally shot drivers in Texas and Maine within six days, according to officials cited by Reuters.
  • Trump plans a prime-time address focused on election claims
    The president said he would address elections; the Associated Press reported that the speech could revisit claims about the 2020 election that have been rejected by courts and election officials.
  • Supreme Court justices request more security funding from Congress
    Justices Amy Coney Barrett and Elena Kagan told lawmakers that threats against the court have increased and sought a budget increase for judicial security.

Brazil

  • Government studies reciprocal measures in response to possible U.S. tariffs
    Brazilian ministers discussed a reciprocal response as the government anticipated a decision on new U.S. tariffs; officials said negotiations had continued on July 14.
  • Supreme Court restriction on Flávio Bolsonaro's visits to his father draws dispute
    Justice Alexandre de Moraes barred Senator Flávio Bolsonaro from visiting Jair Bolsonaro for 90 days, citing an alleged breach of house-arrest conditions; Flávio called the decision disproportionate.
  • Report says Câmara obscured authorship of R$1.3 billion in amendments
    A study cited by Folha alleged that the Chamber of Deputies did not disclose the authorship of R$1.3 billion in parliamentary amendments; the Chamber's response was not included in the search result.
