The Daily Globe: U.S. and Iran exchange further strikes as regional conflict continues, U.S. strikes on Iran continue and a third U.S. service member is reported killed, and Brazil prepares for 25% U.S. tariff on many imports

Published on 2026-07-21

World

  • U.S. and Iran exchange further strikes as regional conflict continues
    U.S. strikes on Iran continued for a ninth consecutive night, while Iranian attacks killed U.S. service members in the region. The reporting described an ongoing escalation and uncertainty about the conflict's scope.
  • Andy Burnham becomes the United Kingdom's prime minister
    Andy Burnham succeeded Keir Starmer as U.K. prime minister on July 20, becoming the country's seventh prime minister in a decade. Burnham said his government would prioritize political stability.
  • Russia reports large Ukrainian drone attack near Moscow
    Russian authorities said Ukraine launched about 400 drones toward the Moscow region on July 20. The report was published amid the continuing Russia-Ukraine war; the claim was attributed to Russian officials.
  • Nicaragua's president rules out future elections
    Daniel Ortega said at a public event that Nicaragua would not hold future elections, according to reporting on July 20. The statement was presented as a decision to prevent the opposition from taking power.

USA

  • U.S. strikes on Iran continue and a third U.S. service member is reported killed
    U.S. forces conducted another round of strikes on Iran, while U.S. officials reported a third service-member death in the wider Middle East conflict. The developments increased domestic debate about the scope and duration of the operation.
  • Person taken into custody after incendiary device incident near New York federal building
    Smoke and flames were reported outside 26 Federal Plaza in lower Manhattan on July 20. The FBI New York Joint Terrorism Task Force investigated, and authorities said a person was taken into custody; the motive was not established in the available reporting.
  • U.S. suspends funding for U.S. Virgin Islands housing authority after corruption investigation
    The Department of Housing and Urban Development suspended funding after an investigation it said found widespread corruption at the U.S. Virgin Islands' housing authority. HUD said the decision affected assistance intended for residents recovering from past hurricanes.
  • Leadership vacancy reported at Justice Department immigration-court office
    Daren Margolin stepped down as director of the Justice Department's Executive Office for Immigration Review, which oversees the immigration-court system. He cited longstanding back problems and did not identify a successor in the reported email.

Brazil

  • Brazil prepares for 25% U.S. tariff on many imports
    The United States is scheduled to apply a 25% tariff to many Brazilian imports beginning July 22, citing what it calls unfair trade practices. Brazil's government called the measure unjustifiable and said it could impose reciprocal tariffs.
  • Lula's party coalition reinforces his 2026 presidential campaign
    The PDT confirmed support for President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva's presidential candidacy at its national convention on July 20. Lula criticized U.S. Secretary of State Marco Rubio in remarks focused on national sovereignty.
  • TSE reports 158.7 million eligible voters for Brazil's 2026 election
    Data cited from Brazil's electoral authority showed 158.7 million people eligible to vote in 2026, a 1.5% increase from 2022. The share of voters aged 60 or older rose from 21% to 23.6% of the electorate.
  • Flávio Bolsonaro's campaign may enter its convention without a vice-presidential nominee
    The coordinator of Flávio Bolsonaro's campaign said the ticket may remain without a vice-presidential nominee at the July 25 convention. The campaign was reported to be seeking an alliance with the Republicanos party.
