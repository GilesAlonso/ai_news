  • FIFA clears USMNT's Balogun to play after reported Trump call to Infantino
    FIFA reversed Folarin Balogun's red-card suspension after a source told Fox News that US President Donald Trump called FIFA President Gianni Infantino to request a review. Belgium's football federation said it is exploring its options following the decision. The episode has prompted debate over political involvement in football governance.
  • Candace Owens alleges betrayal in Charlie Kirk security team; peers push back
    Candace Owens posted allegations that members of Charlie Kirk's security team shared real-time updates with Ben Shapiro's team while Kirk was being transported to hospital after being shot. Liz Wheeler and others responded that security teams routinely coordinate during major incidents, calling the claims unfounded. The dispute has fueled online debate about information flows after Kirk's killing.

USA

  • Sen. Rand Paul renews push to end birthright citizenship via constitutional amendment
    Senator Rand Paul announced a constitutional amendment to end birthright citizenship for children of foreign nationals, tying the proposal to the SAVE America Act. Paul urged constituents to contact senators in support. The measure is opposed by Democrats and immigrant-rights groups, while supporters frame it as a response to illegal immigration.
  • Bipartisan ROAD to Housing Act becomes law without Trump's signature
    The ROAD to Housing Act took effect after President Donald Trump neither signed nor vetoed the bill within the 10-day deadline, allowing it to become law. Senator Elizabeth Warren described the legislation as a measure to expand housing supply and restrict private equity single-family home purchases. Trump withheld his signature in protest of the failed SAVE America Act.
  • Federal Reserve paper ties home-price and rent increases to immigration surge
    A Federal Reserve research paper found that the 2021-2024 immigration surge correlated with a roughly 30 percent rise in home prices and a 20 percent rise in rents. The New York Post and other outlets reported on the findings, citing them in renewed debates over immigration policy under both the Biden and Trump administrations.

Brazil

  • Milei confirms trip to Brazil to back Flávio Bolsonaro's presidential bid
    Argentina's President Javier Milei confirmed he will travel to Brazil to support Flávio Bolsonaro's 2026 presidential campaign. Revista Oeste reported the announcement, which has drawn attention given Milei's prior clashes with Lula and the ideological distance between Milei and much of Brazil's political establishment.
  • PP and União Brasil distance themselves from Flávio Bolsonaro's campaign
    Senator Ronaldo Caiado and allied parties including PP and União Brasil have publicly distanced themselves from Flávio Bolsonaro's presidential bid, citing internal disputes. G1 reported the fractures, which analysts describe as significant defections from the Bolsonaro-aligned bloc less than three months before the 2026 election.
  • Lula calls electoral spending rules 'disgraceful nonsense' and refuses to scale back events
    President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva publicly rejected electoral spending restrictions and declined to scale back government-funded events three months before the 2026 election. Critics argue the rules were strictly enforced against Jair Bolsonaro, while supporters say existing restrictions unfairly target the incumbent. The dispute has intensified debate over electoral fairness and the use of state machinery in campaigning.
  • Brazilian government declines to address alleged US military-action reports
    Brazil's federal government has not publicly responded to reports alleging that the United States could take military action on Brazilian soil. Revista Oeste reported on the silence, which has fueled opposition criticism and conspiracy theories on X. Officials have neither confirmed nor denied the underlying reporting.
