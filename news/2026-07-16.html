Published on 2026-07-16

World

  • Iran threatens to restrict Middle East energy exports amid renewed U.S. pressure
    Associated Press reported on July 15 that Iran threatened to cut off Middle East energy exports, while the United States reimposed a blockade on Iranian ports after reported Iranian attacks in the Strait of Hormuz.
  • Argentina reaches the 2026 World Cup final after defeating England 2–1
    Argentina came from behind to defeat England in Atlanta, with Enzo Fernández and Lautaro Martínez scoring late goals; Argentina will face Spain in the final.
  • European Union reiterates support for Ukraine during Kyiv visit
    European Commission President Ursula von der Leyen visited Kyiv and pledged continued military and financial support for Ukraine as its full-scale war with Russia continued.

USA

  • Senate hearing questions Todd Blanche on attorney general nomination
    Senators questioned acting Attorney General Todd Blanche about prosecutorial independence, the handling of government documents concerning Jeffrey Epstein, and a proposed fund for January 6 rioters.
  • ICE ordered to suspend most vehicle stops as debate over enforcement continues
    The administration ordered U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement to suspend most vehicle stops after shootings and deaths connected to enforcement operations; President Donald Trump said ICE should continue traffic stops.
  • House votes to make daylight saving time permanent
    The U.S. House passed legislation that would make daylight saving time permanent, advancing a long-running dispute over seasonal clock changes.

Brazil

  • United States announces 25% tariffs on most Brazilian imports
    The U.S. announced that a 25% tariff on most Brazilian goods would take effect July 22 after a year-long investigation into what Washington described as unfair trade practices; Brazil rejected the allegations.
  • Brazil disputes U.S. claims about trade practices and anti-corruption enforcement
    Brazilian officials said the country had not engaged in the unfair practices cited by the U.S. Trade Representative, turning the tariff announcement into a bilateral policy dispute.
  • Additional U.S. forced-labor tariff review remains pending
    A separate U.S. investigation into Brazil’s enforcement against forced labor could lead to an additional 12.5% duty; a decision was expected the following week.
